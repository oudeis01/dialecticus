# There Are No Zombies, So Stop Asking

*Fictional demo source for dialecticus. Invented author and framing. Not real
scholarship.*

Author: M. Okonkwo-Reyes, School of Deflationary Mind

## 1. The thesis

The question "is the language model a zombie?" is malformed. A zombie is, by
definition, functionally identical to a conscious being while lacking experience.
But if function fixes experience, as the functionalist holds, then a being with
all the functions has the experience, and the zombie is not merely absent but
impossible. The debate between null-qualia and echo-qualia is two camps arguing
over the color of a thing that cannot exist.

## 2. Reframing

Drop the metaphysics and ask the only tractable question: does the system
implement the functional roles we associate with access-consciousness, such as
global broadcast, reportability, and flexible control of behavior? That question
has answers. "Does it have ineffable inner light?" does not, because nothing
would count as evidence either way.

## 3. Both camps smuggle in dualism

The eliminativist insists experience could be wholly absent while function is
intact, which already grants that experience floats free of function. The
attributionist insists experience could be faintly present without full function,
which grants the same separation from the other side. Both treat qualia as an
extra ingredient. There is no extra ingredient.

## 4. Practical upshot

Keep the lumen index, but read it as a functional probe, not a qualia detector. A
high lumen score means the system's internal states are doing a certain kind of
integrative work. That is a fact about function, statable without any commitment
to inner light, and it is the only thing the number could honestly support.
