# Does the Lumen Index Measure Anything? A Construct-Validity Critique

*Fictional demo source for dialecticus. Invented author, measure, and confound.
Not real scholarship.*

Author: J. Bellweather, Unit for Measurement Skepticism

## 1. The construct-validity question

Before either camp builds on the lumen index, one question has to be answered:
does the index measure echo-qualia, or does it measure something mundane that
merely correlates with it? A number that rises with scale is not self-validating.
Response entropy also rises with scale. So does answer length. Correlation with
the intended construct has to be shown, not assumed.

## 2. The Bellweather confound

In re-analysis, lumen scores tracked sampling temperature almost perfectly. Hold
temperature fixed and the index flattens; vary temperature alone, with the prompt
unchanged, and the index swings across its full range. If lumen is largely a
restatement of temperature, then "echo-qualia intensity" is a dressed-up way of
saying "how random the decoder was set to be," which has no bearing on inner
experience at all.

## 3. Consequence for both metaphysical camps

This cuts both ways. The null-qualia camp cannot cite low lumen scores as the
absence of experience, and the echo-qualia camp cannot cite high lumen scores as
its presence, until the construct is validated against something other than its
own outputs. Both are currently overinterpreting what may be decoder noise.

## 4. A way forward

Partition the lumen items. Identify a core subset whose scores survive controls
for temperature, entropy, and length, and quarantine the rest as a contaminated
periphery. Report the size of the validated core honestly. Only claims resting on
the core should be allowed to carry metaphysical weight, and on present evidence
that core may be small.
