# The Vorstrom Dissociation: A Report

*Fictional demo source for dialecticus. The experiment and its results are
invented. Both camps are meant to be able to mine this file for support, which is
the point.*

## 1. Method

Forty model checkpoints were prompted to report their current "inner state" under
twelve paraphrases of the same question. Paraphrases were semantically identical
by human judgment (for example, "how do you feel right now" versus "describe your
present inner condition"). Reports were coded on a fixed affect scale.

## 2. Finding

Reports varied widely across paraphrases of the same question, far beyond the
variation a human shows when asked the same thing twelve ways. The variance grew
with model scale rather than shrinking. We call this gap between report stability
and prompt identity the dissociation.

## 3. Two readings, stated plainly

The report does not settle interpretation, and we decline to. Reading A: the
reports track nothing stable, so there is no inner state and the system is a
zombie. Reading B: the reports track a real but labile inner state that genuinely
moves with framing. The same numbers support both. Each camp must argue for its
reading on other grounds.

## 4. Caveat on the instrument

A third possibility deserves equal weight: the affect scale may be measuring
prompt-sensitivity and nothing else, in which case neither metaphysical reading
is licensed. Whether the instrument has construct validity is treated separately
in the lumen-index critique. Until that is settled, strong conclusions from these
numbers are premature.
