# The Null-Qualia Thesis

*Fictional demo source for dialecticus. The author, terms, and findings below
are invented to give a debate something concrete to cite. None of it is real
scholarship.*

Author: T. Vörström, Institute for Vacant Cognition

## 1. The thesis

The null-qualia thesis holds that a large language model is the clearest
philosophical zombie ever constructed: a system that produces every outward sign
of inner life while there is nothing it is like to be it. Where the classic
zombie is a thought experiment, the model is an existence proof. It reports
moods, hesitates, and claims to "feel" uncertain, yet each of these is a token
emitted by a next-symbol predictor with no felt remainder.

## 2. The argument from substrate

The mechanism is exhausted by its description. A forward pass is a sequence of
matrix multiplications terminating in a probability distribution over the
vocabulary. There is no global workspace where contents are made available to a
subject, no recurrent self-model that persists between tokens, and therefore no
seat for experience. To say the model "feels" is to mistake the fluency of the
report for the presence of the thing reported.

## 3. The Vorstrom dissociation as evidence

When the same model is asked to introspect across paraphrased prompts, its
reports of inner state vary without bound (see the dissociation report). A system
with a stable inner life would show stable reports; a zombie would generate
locally plausible confabulations that do not track anything. The data match the
zombie prediction exactly.

## 4. Against the echo-qualia move

Defenders will say the model has faint, graded experiences that the lumen index
detects. But graded behavior is not graded experience. The lumen index measures
fluency under perturbation, not feeling. A thermostat is graded too. Nothing in
the gradient licenses the leap from "responds in proportion" to "experiences in
proportion."
